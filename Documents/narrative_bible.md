# Elements TD — Narrative Bible

## Purpose
This document establishes the canonical names, world lore, character bios, and story structure for Aurelune. All other documents and in-game text should be consistent with the terms defined here.

---

## 1. The World — Aurelune

**Aurelune** (AW-reh-loon) is a continent-realm defined by four elemental kingdoms arranged around a central capital. The four kingdoms were bound together under a unified crown for generations, kept in balance by the **Alumite Compact** — a system of magical treaties enforced through elemental stones mined across the realm.

Alumite is a rare mineral that absorbs and stabilizes elemental energy. It forms the foundation of both defensive architecture and magical infrastructure across Aurelune. Towers built with Alumite cores naturally channel the elemental energy of the land around them.

The realm was prosperous but militarily fragile. When a corrupting force emerged from beyond the northern wastes, it exploited the prolonged absence of the crown's Warden — and the isolation of the Queen — to fracture the compact from within.

---

## 2. The Four Kingdoms

### Emberveil
**Element:** Fire
**Geography:** Volcanic highlands, forge cities, obsidian roads
**People:** The Embervite — master smiths, forgers, and fire-callers
**Pre-corruption:** An industrial powerhouse; supplied Aurelune's military with arms and fortifications
**Corrupted state:** Hellfire has replaced forge-fire; the furnaces now churn out demonic constructs; the Embervite who remain are enslaved or consumed
**Ruling seat:** The Ashen Throne (a great forge-palace now cracked and burning)

### Thalvere
**Element:** Water
**Geography:** Flooded lowlands, canal cities, drowned keeps, coastal marshes
**People:** The Thalverin — navigators, flood-engineers, and water-shapers
**Pre-corruption:** The breadbasket of Aurelune; also managed the realm's water trade and messenger fleets
**Corrupted state:** Floodgates were opened during the fall; much of the kingdom is submerged; the drowned dead now walk
**Ruling seat:** The Tidespire (a great lighthouse-castle, still visible above the floodline)

### Greymantle
**Element:** Earth
**Geography:** Mountain passes, mining roads, collapsed stone fortresses
**People:** The Greyborn — stonemasons, siege engineers, and earth-callers
**Pre-corruption:** Aurelune's defensive backbone; built the great walls and fortifications of the realm
**Corrupted state:** The mines have become demon foundries; armored constructs march out of old quarries
**Ruling seat:** The Keystone Keep (a fortress dug into the mountain itself)

### Aelcrest
**Element:** Air
**Geography:** High cliff settlements, sky bridges, signal towers
**People:** The Aelvin — scouts, messengers, wind-shapers, and aerial sentinels
**Pre-corruption:** Operated the realm's early warning network via signal towers; kept communication flowing
**Corrupted state:** Signal towers broadcast corrupted messages; sky bridges have fallen; the Aelvin have been scattered
**Ruling seat:** The Gale Spire (a tower so tall its peak is above cloud level)

## 3. The Capital — Solharrow

**Solharrow** (SOL-hah-row) is the capital of Aurelune and the seat of the unified crown. It is built at the geographic and political center of the realm, where all four elemental currents naturally converge. This convergence made it the most magically stable and architecturally magnificent city in the realm.

Solharrow fell not by invasion, but by **corruption from within**. When the Hollow Regent turned Elany, she opened the gates. The city is now occupied, its towers silent, its population scattered or consumed.

The player's ultimate goal is the liberation of Solharrow and the purification of Elany.

## 4. Characters

### Caelan, Warden of Aurelune
**Role:** Player character
**Voice:** Determined, worn, quietly devoted; does not give rousing speeches — speaks with purpose
**Background:**
Caelan served as Aurelune's Warden — the crown's enforcer and field commander — for over a decade. His duty required constant travel across the four kingdoms, resolving disputes, suppressing uprisings, and holding the fragile compact together. He was deeply committed to the realm, and equally devoted to Elany. But the demands of the wardenhood meant long absences. He believed that protecting the realm was protecting her. He was wrong.

When word reached him that Solharrow had fallen, Caelan was in Emberveil on a border enforcement campaign. He returned to find the capital closed and Elany missing. He established the Warden's Refuge on a remote island and began the long fight to reclaim Aurelune.

**Arc:** Caelan begins the campaign believing the mission is military — reclaim the kingdoms, assault the capital, destroy Vorath. As the story progresses, he is forced to confront that the corruption targeted Elany specifically because of her isolation. His grief and guilt sharpen his resolve but also threaten to become a blind spot. His arc ends with him choosing mercy over execution — and that choice being the thing that breaks the Hollow Regent's hold.

**Design rule:** Caelan is not silent protagonist-blank. He has dialogue, opinions, and emotional reactions. But the player should never feel him making choices they disagree with. He is righteous, not righteous-sounding.

---

### Elany, Queen of Aurelune
**Role:** Central narrative figure; antagonist in Phases 4–5, redeemed by the ending
**Voice (pre-corruption):** Warm, sharp-minded, dry humor when comfortable; carries quiet sadness when alone
**Voice (corrupted):** Cold, deliberate, unnervingly calm; speaks in Vorath's cadences but her own words occasionally break through
**Background:**
Elany inherited the crown of Aurelune through a line of remarkable rulers. She was a gifted administrator and a subtle political mind. When Caelan was appointed Warden, their partnership was professional before it became personal. She loved Aurelune and she loved Caelan, but could not make him choose between them — and would not ask him to.

During the extended wardenhood campaigns, Elany governed alone. When Vorath's whispers reached Solharrow, he found a queen who had held everything together for years without being broken — but who was deeply, quietly exhausted. The corruption worked not through force, but through grief weaponized into surrender.

**Design rule:** Elany is not a passive victim. Every corrupted decision she makes has her fingerprints on it — she chose, at the threshold, to let Vorath in. The story does not excuse this. It understands it. Her redemption comes from her reclaiming her own agency, not from Caelan saving her.

---

### Oryn, Archmage of Aurelune
**Role:** Primary companion; mission briefer; camp NPC with the most dialogue
**Voice:** Scholarly, anxious under pressure, genuinely funny when not terrified; has lived too long and seen too much
**Background:**
Oryn served as court archmage under three rulers before Elany. He was in Solharrow when the city fell but escaped through an Alumite-sealed tunnel he had built decades earlier "just in case." He arrived at Caelan's island barely alive, carrying three books, a broken compass, and a great deal of regret.

Oryn provides strategic intelligence, lore exposition, and emotional levity. He is not brave, but he stays. His arc is about choosing to be useful despite his fear.

**Camp role:** Oryn runs the war map, handles research explanations, and delivers mission briefings. He also has long optional dialogue chains that expand world lore and character history.

---

### Vorath, The Hollow Regent
**Role:** Primary antagonist; final boss
**Voice:** Never shouts. Always calm. Speaks as if correcting a child's arithmetic.
**Background:**
Vorath is not the origin of the corruption — he is its most successful instrument. He was once a mortal ruler from beyond the northern wastes who traded everything to the void for power against an enemy that no longer exists. What remained was a being of vast capability and no remaining purpose except the consumption of order.

He calls himself the Hollow Regent because he believes all rulers are hollow at their core — performing authority rather than possessing it. His occupation of Solharrow is not conquest; it is proof of a philosophical point.

**Design rule:** Vorath should never feel like a generic demon overlord. He is not cruel for pleasure. He is indifferent — which is more frightening. The moment he shows contempt is the moment he feels something, and that should be the crack that Caelan exploits.

**Final encounter:** Vorath does not beg or threaten. When the battle turns against him, he expresses something close to surprise — as if he had not seriously considered the possibility of losing. He dissolves without fanfare. The Alumite in the capital stabilizes. Elany is released.

---

### Supporting Camp Characters (Post-MVP)

**Saris** — A former Embervite forge-worker. Practical, low patience for strategy-talk. Manages the Forge upgrade station.

**Lenne** — A Thalverin navigator who reads water-sign currents as weather omens. Nervous and optimistic in equal measure. Arrives at camp after Thalvere is reclaimed.

**Daveth** — A Greyborn stonemason who joins after Greymantle is cleared. Speaks rarely and at length when he does. Repairs the camp's physical infrastructure with increasing ambition.

**Vael** — An Aelvin scout. Youngest of the named NPCs. Fast-talking, easily distracted. Arrives after Aelcrest; becomes the player's tactical scout.

## 5. The Alumite Stone

**Alumite** is the material foundation of Aurelune's magical infrastructure. It is:
- A dark blue-grey mineral with faint internal luminescence
- Found in seams beneath all four kingdom territories
- Capable of absorbing, stabilizing, and redirecting elemental energy
- The reason Aurelune's architecture is so resilient: towers built on Alumite foundations amplify and channel elemental power naturally

**Corruption vector:** Vorath's influence cannot directly destroy Alumite-reinforced structures. Instead, it works by corrupting the elemental flow within the stone — turning it from a stabilizing force to an amplifying one for demon energy. The visual indicator of corrupted Alumite is a deep crimson cracking through the blue-grey stone.

**Gameplay relevance:** The player's tower infusion system works because the towers in each combat map have Alumite cores. Elemental orbs bond with the Alumite and redirect the tower's energy output.

---

## 6. The Hollow Compact (Antagonist Faction)

Vorath does not command a named faction with its own culture. His forces are corrupted versions of things that already existed — Embervite furnace workers, Thalverin flood-engines, Greyborn constructs. This is intentional: the horror is not invasion, it is desecration.

The few elite corrupted units who serve Vorath directly are referred to in lore as **Hollow-Bound** — individuals who surrendered their will at a threshold moment, as Elany did. They are not mindless; they act with intelligence. But they are empty of self.

## 7. Story Beats by Region

### Refuge (Opening)
- Intro: Caelan arrives at the island with Oryn barely alive
- Establishes: Solharrow has fallen, Elany is missing/occupied, the compact is broken
- Tone: Grief and resolve; not despair
- First camp scene: Oryn explains the political situation over a ruined map table

### Emberveil (Region 1)
- Mission 1 opening: Caelan surveys the corrupted Ashen Road; Oryn notes the hellfire is recent
- Mission 3 reveal: Discover that the Embervite forges are being used to manufacture corrupted Alumite cores for Vorath's constructs
- Climax: Destroy The Cinderbound; liberate the Ashen Throne
- Post-region: The first Embervite survivors reach the camp; Saris arrives; Forge station opens
- Emotional beat: Caelan finds a letter Elany wrote to him during one of his long absences; it was never sent

### Thalvere (Region 2)
- Central conflict: The floodgates were opened by a corrupted Thalverin official who genuinely believed Vorath's promises
- Narrative theme: What do people surrender when hope is absent?
- Climax: Close the floodgates; defeat the drowned-lord boss
- Emotional beat: Caelan learns that Elany had sent messengers to find him during the corruption's early stages; they never reached him

### Greymantle (Region 3)
- Central conflict: The Greyborn's siege machines are now marching under Hollow-Bound commanders
- Narrative theme: Tools of protection repurposed into instruments of destruction
- Climax: Collapse the demon foundries beneath Keystone Keep
- Emotional beat: Oryn confesses he saw signs of Elany's vulnerability before the fall but said nothing — he thought it would pass

### Aelcrest (Region 4)
- Central conflict: The signal towers are broadcasting corruption instructions to the other kingdoms; cutting the network is essential before the assault on Solharrow
- Narrative theme: The cost of being the one who carries messages no one wants to hear
- Climax: Destroy the broadcast array at the Gale Spire summit
- Emotional beat: A recovered Aelvin signal scroll reveals that Elany specifically requested Caelan be recalled — the message was intercepted and suppressed by Vorath's agents

### Solharrow (Region 5)
- Tone shift: The city is recognizable but inverted; familiar landmarks are corrupted
- Central conflict: Fight through six missions to reach the Hollow Throne
- Elany encounters: She appears as an antagonist figure in two missions before the final confrontation — her dialogue is cold and deliberate, but players who pay attention will hear her own voice occasionally breaking through
- Final confrontation: Vorath speaks through Elany; Caelan refuses to fight her; the refusal is what breaks Vorath's investment — he needed the betrayal to be complete
- Resolution: Elany chooses to expel the Hollow Compact; it costs her the crown (not her life); the realm begins to heal

## 9. Tone Guidelines for Writing

- **Do not use chosen one language.** Caelan is competent, not destined.
- **Grief is present but not paralyzing.** Characters feel the losses; they keep moving.
- **Vorath does not monologue.** He says one thing, correctly, and leaves.
- **Elany's corruption is never used for shock value.** Her appearances should be uncomfortable in a specific, personal way — not grotesque.
- **The camp is safe but not happy.** The NPCs find moments of warmth; they do not pretend the war is not happening.
- **Restoration is the theme, not revenge.** Caelan never says he wants to kill Vorath. He wants to take his home back.
